New Dimensions in Energy Materials
主讲人 : Duncan H. Gregory
With the steady depletion of fossil fuels, concerns over environmental issues and the necessity for secure sources of fuel supply, the need to explore alternative sources of energy is becoming more urgent. This situation presents a fundamental challenge for chemistry and for materials chemistry especially since not only are means to produce electricity or fuel sustainably required, but we will also require new materials to convert and store this energy effectively. One can thus consider storing electrical energy directly (for example in batteries) or indirectly using an energy vector – such as hydrogen. Moreover, we can harvest or capture available or waste energy using devices such as thermoelectric generators to convert heat into electricity. Our research at Glasgow is concerned with many of these themes at the interface of materials chemistry and energy. This talk will give an overview of aspects of this research and consider how one might design and produce new materials that can, for example, store clean fuels, have high ionic conductivity and/or high lithium storage capacity or convert heat into electricity efficiently. It will also consider how one might synthesise and process such materials more sustainably. The importance of nano-design strategies in developing new sustainable energy materials is central to this work.
主讲人简介：
Prof. Duncan H. Gregory FIMMM, FRSC
Prof. Gregory is the WestCHEM chair of Inorganic Materials, University of Glasgow. He was previously an EPSRC Advanced Fellow, Lecturer then Reader in Materials Chemistry at the University of Nottingham. He is currently a Visiting Professor at Kyushu University, was Vice President of the RSC Materials Chemistry Division Council from 2009-2014 and was the recipient of the RSC Sustainable Energy Award in 2009. His research interests focus on the synthesis and characterization of new solids including sustainable energy materials (e.g. Li batteries, fuel storage, thermoelectrics), inorganic nanomaterials and the solid-state chemistry of nitrides and chalcogenides. His research also embraces the sustainable production of materials including the microwave synthesis and processing of solids. 含液多孔介质液固耦合动力分析的物质点方法
主讲人 : 大连理工大学郑勇刚教授
含液多孔介质是由固体骨架和孔隙流体组成的混合介质，其力学特性和行为对地下资源的开采与开发、土木工程结构设计与安全评估、动植物生长和仿生器件的调控与设计等都具有非常重要的影响，相关力学行为分析是解决众多工程问题的基础。近年来，本课题组围绕含液多孔介质液固耦合动力行为分析的粒子类物质点方法开展了一系列研究工作。针对含液多孔介质液固耦合大变形及接触行为分析，提出了基于欧拉和拉格朗日网格相结合的接触算法，建立了统一的物质点求解方法。针对传统物质点方法在分析瞬态载荷作用下含液多孔介质时程响应时数值振荡较大这一问题，发展了时间间断物质点方法，提高了计算精度。针对孔隙尺度的含液多孔介质液固耦合行为分析，提出了基于粒子域的液固界面追踪算法，发展了物质点-格子玻尔兹曼耦合算法等。


主讲人简介：
郑勇刚，大连理工大学教授，博士生导师，现任大连理工大学工程力学系副主任。2002年和2008年毕业于大连理工大学工程力学系，分别获工学学士和博士学位。2005年和2008年分别赴澳大利亚悉尼大学和香港理工大学访问交流，任访问学者和研究助理，2008年起在大连理工大学任教。入选中组部“万人计划”青年拔尖人才、教育部“新世纪优秀人才支持计划”、“兴辽英才计划”青年拔尖人才。科研方向为非均质固体材料及含液孔隙介质力学行为的多尺度分析模型与计算方法。累计发表SCI收录论文80余篇，他引900余次。多次应邀在国内外学术会议上做邀请报告和Keynote报告，主持国家自然科学基金项目3项。曾获全国优秀博士学位论文提名奖、ICACM Young Investigator Award和杜庆华工程计算方法优秀青年学者奖等。
