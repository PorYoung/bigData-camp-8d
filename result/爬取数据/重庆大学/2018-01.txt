Air-cathode Microbial Fuel Cell for Domestic Wastewater Treatment
主讲人 : Taeho Lee 教授
The study introduces a novel microbial electrochemical technology to enhance nitrogen removal instead of high power production from domestic wastewater. A flat-panel air-cathode microbial Fuel cell (FA-MFC) successfully treated domestic wastewater to satisfy effluent quality standards of chemical oxygen demand (COD) and total nitrogen (TN) within a very short hydraulic retention time (HRT) of 2.5 h. The FA-MNC system allowed simultaneous removals of COD and TN from domestic wastewater, although it led to minimal power output. Illumina MiSeq revealed that various bacterial communities involved in nitrification, denitrification, fermentation, sulfate reduction, and iron reduction were discovered in the FA-MNC system. Four possible mechanisms for simultaneous nitrification and denitrification have been identified: (1) ammonia and nitrite oxidation using oxygen penetrated through the cathode, (2) heterotrophic denitrification at both the anode and cathode, (3) anaerobic ammonium oxidation in the anaerobic zone, and (4) autotrophic denitrification at the anode. The FA-MNC system can be an innovative and sustainable domestic wastewater treatment process.

翟季冬报告摘要：
图数据有着强大的表达能力，在社交网络、生物信息、网页搜索等领域发挥着重要作用。现实生活中的图数据规模高速增长，例如中文网页图的链接数在2017年初达到了十万亿规模。庞大的图数据规模，以及其复杂的数据依赖、不规则的特点，给高效的图计算提出了很大的挑战。针对上述问题，我们在世界Top500排名第一的超级计算机——神威太湖之光上研究了图计算中一个广泛应用的核心算法，宽度优先搜索。针对太湖之光异构处理器的体系结构特点，提出了一系列优化技术，有效地提高了该算法在超大规模高性能计算机上的可扩展性。我们优化的宽度优先搜索算法能够有效扩展到一千万处理器核以上，并在Graph500排名中达到了异构高性能计算机第一、全榜第二的结果。
李超报告摘要：
大数据时代日益强调高性能、高效率、可扩展的计算系统。在传统体系结构优化收益递减的情况下，跨越不同层次的资源管理对实现智能计算日益重要。本次报告介绍我们在图大数据、神经网络、内存计算等若干代表性应用场合下的跨层次计算资源管理，旨在探讨一种更高效的异构计算资源利用方式。
陆游游报告摘要：
随着系统规模的快速增长，基于磁盘的存储系统在性能以及能耗上面临越来越严峻的挑战。闪存固态盘等非易失性存储提供了新的机遇。然而，由于磁盘作为外存的主要设备已经历70余年，现有的存储系统过多地针对磁盘特性进行设计与优化，难以发挥闪存的优势。本报告首先探讨面向闪存的存储系统在存储结构上的变革，介绍基于裸闪存设备（也被称为Open-Channel SSD）的软件直管闪存结构。接着从文件系统存储管理、命名空间管理及并发感知的数据布局等多个方面介绍闪存文件系统的研究进展。最后，介绍分布式闪存存储系统的设计，并总结基于闪存的存储系统设计中的机遇与难点。

Mechanical characterization of nanosized materials and structures
主讲人 : 闫橙
Nano-sized materials and structures have demonstrated unique properties, with potential for broad applications include lightweight structures, microelectronics, energy harvesting and storage, bio-devices and tissue engineering. The structure-property relations in these materials and structures have not been well understood, which are attracting increasing research effort. On the other hand, advanced energy systems with enhanced conversion efficiencies, improved storage capacities and better reliabilities are being developed to meet the global energy needs. Lithium ion battery technology is increasingly used in portable electronic devices and electric vehicles owing to its high energy density and long lifespan. In this presentation, our recent work on modeling and mechanical characterization of nano carbon, thin films,  l-organic  work (MOF), biomedical scaffolds and electrode materials will be briefly introduced.
